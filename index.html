Stellar Populations

I work on the Andromeda Galaxy at Queen's University with Stéphane Courteau. I did my undergraduate work at Rice University in Houston, Texas (with Prof Dufour). I also did summer research on the GHOSTS nearby galaxy survey with Roelof de Jong at the Space Telescope Science Institute in Baltimore, MD.

Going further back, I represented my country twice at the Intel International Science & Engineering Fair, winning an Intel Foundation grand award. At the Canada Wide Science Fair, I won the Top Junior and Top Intermediate-aged project in consecutive years.

ANDROIDS

I'm the Principal Investigator of the Andromeda Optical & Infrared Disk Survey (coyly dubbed ANDROIDS). We're using the WIRCam and MegaCam instruments on the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope to assemble next generation maps of Andromeda's stellar populations and structure. The first ANDROIDS paper, now in the late stages of preparation, discusses our assembly of the deepest, widest, and most highest-resolution surface brightness maps of the entire M31 disk to date. Near-infrared imaging of Andromeda is imperilled not only by a bright and variable sky glow, but also by rapid changes within WIRCam itself.

More broadly, ANDROIDS can be thought of as the whole-disk counterpart to the Panchromatic Hubble Andromeda Treasury Survey (PHAT, PI Dalcanton). Although CFHT cannot match the supreme depth and clarity of Hubble imaging, the similar wavelength domain of ANDROIDS will help us extrapolate PHAT's insights across the entire disk of Andromeda.

The ANDROIDS team consists of myself (Queen's; Principal Investigator), Stéphane Courteau (Queen's; PhD Advisor), Jean-Charles Cuillandre (CFHT; MegaCam Guru) with contributions from M. McDonald (MIT), Roelof de Jong (IAP), Julianne Dalcanton (UW) and Brent Tully (UH IfA).

Code

Mo’Astro

Derived from MongoDB astronomy, Mo’Astro is a framework for building observational astronomy pipelines around the schema-less MongoDB data store. With Mo’Astro, managing the metadata and pipeline state for thousands of observations becomes a bit easier. Mo’Astro includes hooks into the Astromatic pipeline software.

Ghostmap

Delaunay and Voronoi tessellations for astronomy.

Delphinus

Python helpers for the DOLPHOT photometry software by Andrew Dolphin.

pySPS

Python interface to the Flexible Stellar Population Synthesis Package by Conroy, Gunn and White.

MapScale

Embarassingly parallel processing with the ZeroMQ message-passing framework. Replaces for multiprocessing’s Pool.map().
