Stellar Populations

I’m a PhD candidate in astrophysics at Queen’s University, Canada. I work under St√©phane Courteau on understanding the stellar populations and structure of nearby galaxies. In particular, I am the principle investigator of ANDROIDS, a survey of the Andromeda Galaxy’s light from the near-ultraviolet to the near-infrared using MegaCam and WIRCam on the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope.

I did my undergraduate work at Rice University in Houston, Texas. There, I did a thesis examining the photoionization structure of the Eskimo Nebula with Prof Reginal Dufour. I also did summer research on the GHOSTS nearby galaxy survey with Roelof de Jong at the Space Telescope Science Institute in Baltimore, MD.

Going further back, I represented my country twice at the Intel International Science & Engineering Fair, where my honours included an Intel Foundation grand award. At the Canada Wide Science Fair, I won the Top Junior and Top Intermediate-aged project in consecutive years.

Code

Mo’Astro

Derived from MongoDB astronomy, Mo’Astro is a framework for building observational astronomy pipelines around the schema-less MongoDB data store. With Mo’Astro, managing the metadata and pipeline state for thousands of observations becomes a bit easier. Mo’Astro includes hooks into the Astromatic pipeline software.

Ghostmap

Delaunay and Voronoi tessellations for astronomy.

Daopilot

Python drivers for the DAOPHOT/ALLSTAR suite of photometry software by Peter Stetson.

pySPS

Python interface to the Flexible Stellar Population Synthesis Package by Conroy, Gunn and White.

MapScale

Embarassingly parallel processing with the ZeroMQ message-passing framework. Replaces for multiprocessing’s Pool.map().
